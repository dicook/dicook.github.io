Spending for House Races

in Contested Districts

 
Candidates for the US House of Representatives, in the top 50 races (by total amount spent by both campaigns) that have two candidates in the general election. We see that there is a clear incumbent advantage (candidates that have already been elected once tend to spend more money). Due to redistricting, several districts have two incumbents (for instance, the Iowa 3rd district, and the California 30th district). California has a unique system for determining general election candidates: the top two candidates of any party in an open primary run against each other in the general election. This means that in some districts (CA-30) there are two members of the same party competing for the seat.
